# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Teams that need a dependable way to turn audio or video files into usable SRT subtitle files. They may be producing training videos, marketing clips, internal recordings, course material, webinars, or other recurring media where subtitle accuracy and delivery speed matter.

They come to the interface with a practical job: upload a media file, generate subtitles, download the result, and trust that the process is clear enough to use repeatedly without extra explanation.

## Product Purpose

The product exists to make subtitle generation fast, predictable, and low-friction. Success means a team member can upload an audio or video file, understand what is happening during processing, and receive a correctly named SRT file without needing technical knowledge.

The interface should earn trust through clarity, reliable states, privacy and processing confidence, and a workflow that feels professional rather than experimental.

## Brand Personality

Professional, reliable, minimal.

The tone should be direct and calm. It should feel like a focused utility built for real team workflows: efficient, legible, and confident without trying to entertain the user.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic AI SaaS patterns: vague gradient blobs, inflated claims, buzzword-heavy copy, and decorative "AI magic" visuals.

Avoid toy upload-tool styling: cramped spacing, cheap-looking controls, unclear states, and hobby-project polish.

Avoid flashy creator-app energy: loud colors, trend-driven visuals, excessive motion, and anything that makes the workflow feel less trustworthy.

## Design Principles

Prioritize the job. Every visible element should help the user choose a file, understand processing, or retrieve the subtitle output.

Make reliability visible. Loading, success, failure, file validation, and download states should be explicit and reassuring.

Use restraint to build trust. Favor calm hierarchy, precise spacing, consistent controls, and minimal decoration.

Respect repeat use. The workflow should be quick to scan, keyboard-friendly, and predictable for people who may use it many times.

Write plainly. Copy should explain what will happen, what is happening, and what the user can do next without hype.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA as the baseline.

Support keyboard operation, visible focus states, strong color contrast, accessible labels, live status messaging for processing feedback, and reduced-motion preferences. Do not rely on color alone to communicate upload, error, loading, or success states.
